# CS 250 Portfolio – SNHU Travel Project

This repository contains my Sprint Review and Retrospective document from the SNHU Travel project. The document summarizes my experiences in Scrum roles, how Agile helped complete user stories, handling changes, communication, tools, and evaluation of Agile vs. Waterfall.

## Essential Questions Reflection

### How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
I interpret user needs by listening to stakeholders and users during interviews or focus groups. I turn their wants into user stories with the format “As a [user] I want [goal] so that [benefit].” This keeps the focus on value and makes requirements clear. User stories help the team understand who the feature is for and why it matters so we build the right thing.

### How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
I approach development by breaking work into small user stories and working in short sprints. I use backlog refinement to clarify requirements before coding and daily stand-ups to stay aligned. In the future I hope to use story points for estimation, planning poker for team consensus, and a Definition of Done to ensure quality. These processes make development predictable and adaptable.

### What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
A good team member communicates openly, shares knowledge, and helps others when needed. They respect roles, participate in events, and focus on delivering working software together. They own the team’s success and adapt to changes without blame. Being collaborative and reliable builds trust and makes the team stronger.
